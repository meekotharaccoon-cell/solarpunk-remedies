## Overview

**Plants used:** elderberry (Sambucus nigra)
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Best season:** late summer to early fall (harvest), year-round (use stored syrup)

## What it helps with

- Immune support
- Cold and flu prevention
- Antioxidant boost

## Basic preparation

1. Harvest ripe elderberries in late summer (dark purple, not red).
2. Remove berries from stems using a fork.
3. Combine 1 cup dried elderberries (or 2 cups fresh) with 3 cups water in a saucepan.

## Safety notes

Never consume raw elderberries, bark, or leaves -- they contain cyanogenic glycosides. Always cook berries before use. Not recommended for pregnant or nursing women without consulting a healthcare provider. May interact with immunosuppressant medications.
