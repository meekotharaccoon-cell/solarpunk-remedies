## Overview

**Plants used:** comfrey (Symphytum officinale)
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Best season:** summer (harvest), year-round (stored salve)

## What it helps with

- Bruise healing
- Sprain support
- Sore muscles
- Bone knitting support

## Basic preparation

1. Harvest comfrey leaves in summer when the plant is in full growth.
2. Wash and dry leaves thoroughly (any moisture will cause mold in the oil).
3. Chop dried leaves and fill a jar halfway.

## Safety notes

FOR EXTERNAL USE ONLY -- comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids that are toxic to the liver if ingested. Never apply to broken skin or open wounds, as compounds can be absorbed. Limit use to 10 days at a time. Not for use during pregnancy.
