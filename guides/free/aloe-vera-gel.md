## Overview

**Plants used:** aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Best season:** year-round (houseplant or warm climate outdoor)

## What it helps with

- Burn care (minor)
- Sunburn relief
- Skin moisturizer
- Wound healing support
- After-shave soothing

## Basic preparation

1. Select a thick, mature outer leaf from a healthy aloe plant (at least 3 years old).
2. Cut the leaf at the base with a clean, sharp knife.
3. Stand the leaf upright in a cup for 10-15 minutes to let the yellow latex (aloin) drain out.

## Safety notes

For external use only unless using food-grade inner gel. The yellow latex between skin and gel is a strong laxative -- drain it fully before use. Do not apply to deep wounds or severe burns. Some people are allergic -- do a patch test first. Do not use on surgical wounds.
