## Overview

**Plants used:** yarrow (Achillea millefolium)
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Best season:** summer (harvest), year-round (dried)

## What it helps with

- Wound care
- Stopping minor bleeding
- Bruise treatment
- Nosebleeds

## Basic preparation

1. Identify yarrow by its feathery, fern-like leaves and flat-topped white flower clusters.
2. Harvest aerial parts (leaves and flowers) during peak bloom.
3. For immediate field use: crush fresh leaves and press directly onto a clean wound.

## Safety notes

For minor wounds only -- seek medical attention for deep or serious wounds. Avoid if allergic to Asteraceae family. May increase skin sensitivity to sunlight. Not for use during pregnancy as it may stimulate uterine contractions. Do not use on infected wounds.
