## Overview

**Plants used:** white willow (Salix alba), crack willow (Salix fragilis)
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Best season:** spring (bark harvest), year-round (dried bark)

## What it helps with

- Headache relief
- Back pain
- Mild pain management
- Fever reduction
- Anti-inflammatory

## Basic preparation

1. Harvest willow bark in spring when the sap is flowing and bark peels easily.
2. Select branches 2-3 inches in diameter from a healthy tree.
3. Score the bark lengthwise and peel it off in strips.

## Safety notes

Contains salicin (similar to aspirin). Do NOT give to children or teenagers (Reye's syndrome risk). Avoid if allergic to aspirin. May interact with blood thinners, NSAIDs, and seizure medications. Not recommended during pregnancy or while breastfeeding. Consult a doctor if taking other medications.
