## Overview

**Plants used:** mullein (Verbascum thapsus)
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Best season:** first-year leaves best in summer, year-round (dried)

## What it helps with

- Respiratory support
- Cough relief
- Bronchial soothing
- Lung tonic

## Basic preparation

1. Identify mullein by its large, soft, fuzzy gray-green leaves and tall flower stalk.
2. Harvest leaves during the first year of growth (before the flower stalk appears) for best potency.
3. Dry leaves on screens or hang in bundles for 1-2 weeks.

## Safety notes

Always strain thoroughly to remove fine hairs. Generally considered very safe. Mullein seeds are mildly toxic -- use only leaves and flowers. May interact with diuretic medications.
