## Overview

**Plants used:** purple coneflower (Echinacea purpurea), narrow-leaf coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia)
**Difficulty:** intermediate
**Best season:** fall (root harvest), summer (aerial parts), year-round (tincture)

## What it helps with

- Cold and flu support
- Immune stimulation
- Upper respiratory infection

## Basic preparation

1. Harvest echinacea roots in fall after the plant is at least 3 years old, or use fresh flowers and leaves.
2. Wash roots thoroughly and chop into small pieces.
3. Fill a glass jar 1/3 to 1/2 full with chopped roots (or 2/3 with fresh aerial parts).
