## Overview

**Plants used:** garden sage (Salvia officinalis)
**Difficulty:** beginner
**Best season:** summer (harvest fresh), year-round (dried)

## What it helps with

- Sore throat relief
- Mouth sores
- Gum inflammation
- Laryngitis

## Basic preparation

1. Harvest sage leaves before or during early flowering.
2. Use 2 tablespoons fresh sage leaves (or 1 tablespoon dried) per cup.
3. Pour boiling water over the sage.

## Safety notes

For gargling only -- do not swallow large amounts. Sage contains thujone which can be toxic in large doses if ingested. Not recommended for prolonged internal use during pregnancy. Safe for occasional gargling in most adults.
