## Overview

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Best season:** fall (root harvest), summer (aerial parts), year-round (tincture)
**Yield:** approximately 1 pint
**Shelf life:** 3-5 years in dark glass

## Plants & Identification

- **purple coneflower (Echinacea purpurea)**
- **narrow-leaf coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest echinacea roots in fall after the plant is at least 3 years old, or use fresh flowers and leaves.

**Step 2.** Wash roots thoroughly and chop into small pieces.

**Step 3.** Fill a glass jar 1/3 to 1/2 full with chopped roots (or 2/3 with fresh aerial parts).

**Step 4.** Cover completely with 80-proof vodka or brandy.

**Step 5.** Seal tightly and label with date and contents.

**Step 6.** Store in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking the jar daily.

**Step 7.** Strain through cheesecloth, squeezing out all liquid.

**Step 8.** Store tincture in dark dropper bottles. Take 1-2 dropperfuls at first sign of cold, up to 3 times daily.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** fall (root harvest), summer (aerial parts), year-round (tincture)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Fall roots contain stored energy and concentrated compounds.
- Harvest after the aerial parts begin to die back.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

Not recommended for people with autoimmune disorders. Do not take continuously for more than 8 weeks. May cause allergic reactions in people sensitive to Asteraceae family plants. Consult a healthcare provider if pregnant or taking immunosuppressants.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
