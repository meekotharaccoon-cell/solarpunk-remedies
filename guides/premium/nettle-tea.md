## Plants & Identification

- **stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Wear thick gloves to harvest young nettle tops in early spring (top 4-6 inches).

**Step 2.** Rinse thoroughly -- cooking or drying neutralizes the sting.

**Step 3.** To dry: spread on screens in a warm, ventilated area for 1-2 weeks.

**Step 4.** Use 1-2 tablespoons dried nettle per cup of water.

**Step 5.** Pour boiling water over nettle in a cup or teapot.

**Step 6.** Cover and steep for 10-15 minutes for a standard tea (or 4-8 hours for a strong nourishing infusion).

**Step 7.** Strain and drink. Can add honey, lemon, or mint for flavor.

**Step 8.** Drink 2-3 cups daily during allergy season for best results.

## Safety Information

Always wear gloves when handling fresh nettle. May interact with blood thinners, blood pressure medications, and diuretics. Not recommended during pregnancy. Start with small amounts to assess tolerance.

**General safety practices:**
