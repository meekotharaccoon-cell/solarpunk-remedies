## Overview

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best season:** spring through fall (fresh), year-round (dried leaves reconstituted)
**Yield:** single application per poultice
**Shelf life:** use fresh; dried leaves keep 1 year

## Plants & Identification

- **broadleaf plantain (Plantago major)**
- **narrowleaf plantain (Plantago lanceolata)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Identify plantain leaves in your yard (broad ribbed leaves growing in a rosette, not the banana-like fruit).

**Step 2.** Harvest several fresh, clean leaves -- avoid areas treated with pesticides.

**Step 3.** Wash leaves thoroughly under running water.

**Step 4.** Chew or crush the leaves to release the plant juices (chewing works fastest in the field).

**Step 5.** Apply the mashed leaf directly to the bug bite, sting, or minor wound.

**Step 6.** Hold in place with a bandage or cloth wrap.

**Step 7.** Replace with fresh poultice every 2-4 hours.

**Step 8.** For a more prepared version, blend leaves with a small amount of water to form a paste.

- **Expected yield:** single application per poultice
- **Shelf life:** use fresh; dried leaves keep 1 year
- Store in clean, airtight glass containers away from direct sunlight.
- Label everything with contents and date of preparation.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** spring through fall (fresh), year-round (dried leaves reconstituted)

- Spring is prime harvest time. Watch for new growth after the last frost.
- Young plants and fresh shoots have the highest concentration of active compounds.
- Fall roots contain stored energy and concentrated compounds.
- Harvest after the aerial parts begin to die back.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

For external use only. Clean the area before applying. Seek medical attention for severe allergic reactions, deep wounds, or signs of infection. Ensure correct plant identification.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
