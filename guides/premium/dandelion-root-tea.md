## Overview

**Yield:** 1 cup per serving
**Shelf life:** 1 year dried in airtight container

## Plants & Identification

- **dandelion (Taraxacum officinale)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Dig dandelion roots in early spring or late fall when energy is stored in the root.

**Step 2.** Wash roots thoroughly, scrubbing off all soil.

**Step 3.** Chop roots into small pieces (about 1/4 inch).

**Step 4.** Optional: roast chopped roots at 300F for 2 hours for a coffee-like flavor.

**Step 5.** Use 1-2 tablespoons of chopped root per cup of water.

**Step 6.** Place roots in a saucepan with cold water and bring to a boil.

**Step 7.** Reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes.

**Step 8.** Strain and serve. Add honey or lemon to taste.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** early spring or late fall (harvest), year-round (dried root)

- Spring is prime harvest time. Watch for new growth after the last frost.
- Young plants and fresh shoots have the highest concentration of active compounds.
- Fall roots contain stored energy and concentrated compounds.
- Harvest after the aerial parts begin to die back.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

Avoid if allergic to Asteraceae family plants. May interact with lithium, antibiotics, and diuretic medications. Not recommended for people with gallbladder problems or bile duct obstruction. Harvest only from areas free of pesticides and herbicides.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
