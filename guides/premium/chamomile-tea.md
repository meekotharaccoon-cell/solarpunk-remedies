## Overview

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best season:** summer (harvest), year-round (dried)
**Yield:** 1 cup per serving
**Shelf life:** 1 year dried in airtight container

## Plants & Identification

- **German chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest chamomile flowers when petals are fully open and beginning to fold back.

**Step 2.** Dry flowers on a screen in a warm, well-ventilated area for 1-2 weeks.

**Step 3.** Use 1 tablespoon dried flowers (or 2 tablespoons fresh) per cup.

**Step 4.** Pour boiling water over flowers in a cup or teapot.

**Step 5.** Cover and steep for 5-10 minutes (longer steeping increases potency).

**Step 6.** Strain and sweeten with honey if desired.

**Step 7.** Drink 30-60 minutes before bedtime for best sleep support.


## Uses & Applications

## Yield & Storage

- **Expected yield:** 1 cup per serving
- **Shelf life:** 1 year dried in airtight container
- Store in clean, airtight glass containers away from direct sunlight.
- Label everything with contents and date of preparation.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** summer (harvest), year-round (dried)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

Avoid if allergic to plants in the Asteraceae/daisy family. May increase effects of blood thinners and sedatives. Generally recognized as safe for most adults.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
