## Plants & Identification

- **lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest lavender stems when about half the flower buds have opened.

**Step 2.** Bundle and hang upside down to dry for 1-2 weeks in a dark, well-ventilated space.

**Step 3.** Strip dried flowers from stems and lightly crush them.

**Step 4.** Fill a clean glass jar halfway with dried lavender flowers.

**Step 5.** Cover completely with a carrier oil (olive oil or sweet almond oil work well).

**Step 6.** Seal jar and place in a sunny windowsill for 3-4 weeks, shaking daily.

**Step 7.** Strain through cheesecloth into a clean jar.

**Step 8.** Apply to temples for headache relief, or use as a massage oil for stress.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** mid-summer (harvest), year-round (stored oil)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.
