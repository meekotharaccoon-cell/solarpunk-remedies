## Plants & Identification

- **comfrey (Symphytum officinale)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest comfrey leaves in summer when the plant is in full growth.

**Step 2.** Wash and dry leaves thoroughly (any moisture will cause mold in the oil).

**Step 3.** Chop dried leaves and fill a jar halfway.

**Step 4.** Cover with olive oil, leaving 1 inch of headspace.

**Step 5.** Infuse for 4-6 weeks in a warm spot, shaking every few days.

**Step 6.** Strain through cheesecloth, squeezing out all oil.

**Step 7.** In a double boiler, melt 1 ounce beeswax per cup of infused oil.

**Step 8.** Stir combined mixture and pour into tins. Let cool completely. Apply to unbroken skin over bruises and sore areas.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** summer (harvest), year-round (stored salve)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

FOR EXTERNAL USE ONLY -- comfrey contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids that are toxic to the liver if ingested. Never apply to broken skin or open wounds, as compounds can be absorbed. Limit use to 10 days at a time. Not for use during pregnancy.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
