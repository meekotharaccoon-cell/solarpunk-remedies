## Overview

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best season:** summer (harvest fresh), year-round (dried)
**Yield:** 1 gargle session per batch
**Shelf life:** dried sage keeps 1 year

## Plants & Identification

- **garden sage (Salvia officinalis)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest sage leaves before or during early flowering.

**Step 2.** Use 2 tablespoons fresh sage leaves (or 1 tablespoon dried) per cup.

**Step 3.** Pour boiling water over the sage.

**Step 4.** Cover and steep for 15 minutes (longer steeping extracts more tannins for astringent effect).

**Step 5.** Strain and let cool to a comfortably warm temperature.

**Step 6.** Add 1/2 teaspoon salt and optionally 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar.

**Step 7.** Gargle for 30 seconds, spit, and repeat until the cup is empty.

**Step 8.** Use 3-4 times daily at the first sign of sore throat.


## Uses & Applications

- Sore throat relief
- Mouth sores
- Gum inflammation
- Laryngitis

## Yield & Storage

- **Expected yield:** 1 gargle session per batch
- **Shelf life:** dried sage keeps 1 year
- Store in clean, airtight glass containers away from direct sunlight.
- Label everything with contents and date of preparation.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** summer (harvest fresh), year-round (dried)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

For gargling only -- do not swallow large amounts. Sage contains thujone which can be toxic in large doses if ingested. Not recommended for prolonged internal use during pregnancy. Safe for occasional gargling in most adults.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
