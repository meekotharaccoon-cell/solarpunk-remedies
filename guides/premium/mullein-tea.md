## Plants & Identification

- **mullein (Verbascum thapsus)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Identify mullein by its large, soft, fuzzy gray-green leaves and tall flower stalk.

**Step 2.** Harvest leaves during the first year of growth (before the flower stalk appears) for best potency.

**Step 3.** Dry leaves on screens or hang in bundles for 1-2 weeks.

**Step 4.** Use 1-2 tablespoons dried leaf per cup of water.

**Step 5.** Pour boiling water over the mullein leaf.

**Step 6.** Cover and steep for 10-15 minutes.

**Step 7.** IMPORTANT: Strain through a fine cloth or coffee filter to remove tiny leaf hairs (they can irritate the throat).

**Step 8.** Drink 2-3 cups daily for respiratory support. Add honey for sore throat relief.

- Store in clean, airtight glass containers away from direct sunlight.
- Label everything with contents and date of preparation.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** first-year leaves best in summer, year-round (dried)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

Always strain thoroughly to remove fine hairs. Generally considered very safe. Mullein seeds are mildly toxic -- use only leaves and flowers. May interact with diuretic medications.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
