## Overview

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best season:** late summer to early fall (harvest), year-round (use stored syrup)
**Yield:** approximately 2 cups
**Shelf life:** 3 months refrigerated

## Plants & Identification

- **elderberry (Sambucus nigra)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest ripe elderberries in late summer (dark purple, not red).

**Step 2.** Remove berries from stems using a fork.

**Step 3.** Combine 1 cup dried elderberries (or 2 cups fresh) with 3 cups water in a saucepan.

**Step 4.** Add 1 cinnamon stick, 4 whole cloves, and 1 tablespoon fresh grated ginger.

**Step 5.** Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 45 minutes until liquid reduces by half.

**Step 6.** Strain through cheesecloth, pressing berries to extract all juice.

**Step 7.** Let cool to lukewarm, then stir in 1 cup raw honey.

**Step 8.** Store in a glass jar in the refrigerator for up to 3 months.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** late summer to early fall (harvest), year-round (use stored syrup)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Fall roots contain stored energy and concentrated compounds.
- Harvest after the aerial parts begin to die back.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

Never consume raw elderberries, bark, or leaves -- they contain cyanogenic glycosides. Always cook berries before use. Not recommended for pregnant or nursing women without consulting a healthcare provider. May interact with immunosuppressant medications.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
