**Shelf life:** 2 years dried in airtight container

## Plants & Identification

- **white willow (Salix alba)**
- **crack willow (Salix fragilis)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest willow bark in spring when the sap is flowing and bark peels easily.

**Step 2.** Select branches 2-3 inches in diameter from a healthy tree.

**Step 3.** Score the bark lengthwise and peel it off in strips.

**Step 4.** Scrape off the inner bark (cambium layer) -- this is the medicinal part.

**Step 5.** Dry bark strips in a warm, ventilated area for 1-2 weeks.

**Step 6.** Chop or grind dried bark into small pieces.

**Step 7.** Simmer 1-2 teaspoons of dried bark in 1 cup of water for 10-15 minutes (decoction method).

**Step 8.** Strain and drink up to 3 cups daily for pain relief. Tastes bitter -- honey helps.

Not recommended during pregnancy or while breastfeeding. Consult a doctor if taking other medications.

**General safety practices:**

- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
