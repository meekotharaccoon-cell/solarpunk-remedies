## Plants & Identification

- **calendula (Calendula officinalis)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Harvest calendula flowers at midday when fully open and dry.

**Step 2.** Dry flowers completely on screens for 1-2 weeks (moisture causes mold).

**Step 3.** Fill a jar halfway with dried calendula petals.

**Step 4.** Cover with olive oil and let infuse for 4-6 weeks in a sunny spot, shaking daily.

**Step 5.** Strain the infused oil through cheesecloth.

**Step 6.** Gently melt 1 ounce beeswax per cup of infused oil in a double boiler.

**Step 7.** Stir beeswax into the warm infused oil until fully combined.

**Step 8.** Pour into small tins or jars. Let cool completely before capping. Apply to minor wounds, dry skin, or rashes.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** summer (harvest), year-round (stored salve)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

For external use only. Avoid if allergic to Asteraceae family plants. Do not apply to deep or puncture wounds. Discontinue use if irritation occurs. Beeswax alternative: candelilla wax for vegan version.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
