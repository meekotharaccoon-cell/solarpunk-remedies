## Plants & Identification

- **turmeric (Curcuma longa)**
- **black pepper (Piper nigrum)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Combine 1/2 cup turmeric powder with 1 cup water in a saucepan.

**Step 2.** Add 1/3 cup coconut oil or olive oil (fat helps absorption of curcumin).

**Step 3.** Add 1-1/2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper (piperine increases curcumin absorption by 2000%).

**Step 4.** Stir over low heat for 7-10 minutes until a thick paste forms.

**Step 5.** Let cool completely.

**Step 6.** Transfer to a clean glass jar and store in the refrigerator.

**Step 7.** Use 1/2 to 1 teaspoon daily mixed into warm milk (golden milk), smoothies, or food.

**Step 8.** Can also be applied topically as an anti-inflammatory paste for sore joints.

## Safety Information

Turmeric stains everything yellow -- protect clothing and surfaces. May interact with blood thinners, diabetes medications, and stomach acid reducers. High doses may cause digestive upset. Consult a healthcare provider if you have gallbladder issues or are pregnant.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
