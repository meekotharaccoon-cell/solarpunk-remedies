## Plants & Identification

- **aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Select a thick, mature outer leaf from a healthy aloe plant (at least 3 years old).

**Step 2.** Cut the leaf at the base with a clean, sharp knife.

**Step 3.** Stand the leaf upright in a cup for 10-15 minutes to let the yellow latex (aloin) drain out.

**Step 4.** Lay the leaf flat and slice off the spiny edges.

**Step 5.** Carefully fillet the leaf by sliding a knife between the gel and the outer skin.

**Step 6.** Scoop out the clear gel with a spoon.

**Step 7.** Blend briefly for a smooth consistency if desired.

**Step 8.** Apply directly to minor burns, sunburn, or irritated skin. Store extra gel in the refrigerator.

## Safety Information

For external use only unless using food-grade inner gel. The yellow latex between skin and gel is a strong laxative -- drain it fully before use. Do not apply to deep wounds or severe burns. Some people are allergic -- do a patch test first. Do not use on surgical wounds.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
