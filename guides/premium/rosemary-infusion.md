## Plants & Identification

- **rosemary (Salvia rosmarinus)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Clip 3-4 sprigs of fresh rosemary (about 4-6 inches each).

**Step 2.** Rinse gently and pat dry.

**Step 3.** Place sprigs in a teapot or heat-safe jar.

**Step 4.** Pour 2 cups of just-boiled water over the rosemary.

**Step 5.** Cover and steep for 10-15 minutes.

**Step 6.** Strain and serve warm. Add honey and lemon if desired.

**Step 7.** Drink 1-2 cups daily for cognitive support.

**Step 8.** Alternatively, use as a hair rinse for scalp stimulation after cooling.

## Safety Information

Generally safe in culinary amounts. Large medicinal doses may be problematic for people with seizure disorders, high blood pressure, or bleeding disorders. Avoid large doses during pregnancy. May interact with blood thinners and ACE inhibitors.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
