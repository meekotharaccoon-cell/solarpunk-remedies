## Overview

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Best season:** summer (harvest fresh), year-round (dried)
**Yield:** 1 session per batch
**Shelf life:** dried thyme keeps 1 year

## Plants & Identification

- **thyme (Thymus vulgaris)**

Always positively identify plants before harvesting. Use a reliable field guide or consult an experienced forager. When wildcrafting, harvest sustainably — never take more than 1/3 of a plant stand.

## Full Preparation Steps

**Step 1.** Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil.

**Step 2.** Remove from heat and add a large handful of fresh thyme (or 3 tablespoons dried).

**Step 3.** Let steep for 2-3 minutes.

**Step 4.** Place the pot on a stable surface. Sit comfortably in front of it.

**Step 5.** Drape a towel over your head and the pot, creating a tent to trap steam.

**Step 6.** Breathe slowly and deeply through your nose for 5-10 minutes.

**Step 7.** Keep eyes closed to avoid irritation.

**Step 8.** Repeat 2-3 times daily during congestion.

- **Expected yield:** 1 session per batch

- Store in clean, airtight glass containers away from direct sunlight.

## Seasonal Notes

**Best time:** summer (harvest fresh), year-round (dried)

- Harvest in the morning after dew has dried but before the midday heat.
- Flowers are best picked just as they fully open.
- Available year-round, but potency may vary by season.
- Stock up on dried material when the plant is at peak vitality.

## Safety Information

Keep face at least 12 inches from the water to prevent burns. Not suitable for young children due to burn risk. Discontinue if breathing becomes difficult. People with asthma should use with caution as steam may trigger spasms.

**General safety practices:**
- Start with small doses to check for allergic reactions.
- Keep a record of what you take and any effects.
- Do not combine multiple herbal remedies without research.
- Seek professional medical help for serious conditions.
- If symptoms worsen or persist, stop use and consult a doctor.
